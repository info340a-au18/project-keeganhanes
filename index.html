U-District Parking

Topic Overview

“RYD” is a start up in Vancouver WA that acts like an Uber for employees at various businesses around downtown. The service is free due to an agreement with the city to fully fund it. RYD’s vision is to not only to help transport employees to and from work but to also take the general public where they need to go, free of charge. This idea came about after several companies made complaints about how limited parking was for their employees.

The U-District is a busy and populated area of Seattle with very limited parking. More than half of UW’s students live off campus and a decent amount of those students have cars. When the place you usually park is completely full or you’re parking somewhere you have never been before it can be frustrating to find a spot and even cause you to be late to where you’re going. If there was an easy and convenient way to know where the best place to park is and how much it’s going to cost, you would save a lot of time and money by preventing yourself from getting a ticket. Although incorporating a service similar to RYD in Seattle would be much more difficult considering the cities size, there are still ways to help the situation. An application that visually shows you where the best places to park around the U-district are would be extremely helpful. It could show you the spots that are most likely going to be full based on the time and day, as well as if you are going to need to pay or not. Although there are applications and websites that have similar functionality this application would be geared towards the students at UW. We could draw inspirations from other companies while still making it unique and our own.

Potential Applications

  • U-park
    • This applications purpose would be to help the user find the best available parking space in the U-district relative to their location, date and time. There would be benefits to having both a website and an application so that if the user needed to find parking last minute they could do so on their phone. The app could potentially even offer discounts for parking garages or parking spots that are only available to the users of the app.
  • “Help me park”
    • This applications purpose would be to get you in contact with someone who will help you park your car. Imagine you are late to an important event and when you get there you cannot find parking. Someone near you can then be paid to park your car in the nearest available parking space and then update you on where it is via the application.
